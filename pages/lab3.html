Question 3:

The main differentiation for me comes with being able to test your visuals in the early stages. For linear media, I would find it hard to visualize the final camera movements, feel and style of the final project. What I find similar is the research and storyboarding phase. Research is essential for both types of media in the same way as you're gathering knowledge about what you want to communicate. For storyboarding, it can differentiate a bit as with linear media, there is a flow in the story from start to end and the user does not have much control on where they start. However, with non-linear media, the storyboard is created by keeping the user flow in mind and thinking about the variations in what is shown based on user interaction.
